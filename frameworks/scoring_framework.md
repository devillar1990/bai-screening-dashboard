# B.AI Scoring Framework: Value Creation Potential (v4)

## Framework Overview

The v4 Value Creation framework is aligned with the Brightstar.AI Screening Criteria (D2). Value creation is organized into THREE tiers representing increasing levels of AI-driven transformation:

1. **Cost Optimization** — AI interventions that reduce operating costs
2. **Revenue Optimization** — AI interventions that improve existing revenue efficiency
3. **Revenue Transformation** — AI interventions that create entirely new revenue streams or fundamentally change the revenue model

## Scoring Scale

Each of the 7 Value Creation categories is scored on TWO dimensions:

**AI Score (1-5):** Incremental value creation potential from AI-specific interventions.
- **1 = Minimal**: No material AI impact (<1pp EBITDA margin improvement)
- **2 = Low**: Limited AI improvement potential (1-3pp EBITDA margin improvement)
- **3 = Moderate**: Meaningful AI improvement potential (3-5pp EBITDA margin improvement)
- **4 = High**: Significant AI improvement potential (5-8pp EBITDA margin improvement)
- **5 = Transformative**: Major AI improvement potential (>8pp EBITDA margin improvement)

**Non-AI Score (1-5):** Value creation potential from traditional PE / operational levers (without AI).
- **1 = Minimal**: No material improvement potential through traditional interventions (<1pp EBITDA margin improvement)
- **2 = Low**: Limited traditional improvement potential (1-3pp EBITDA margin improvement)
- **3 = Moderate**: Meaningful traditional improvement potential (3-5pp EBITDA margin improvement)
- **4 = High**: Significant traditional improvement potential (5-8pp EBITDA margin improvement)
- **5 = Transformative**: Major traditional improvement potential (>8pp EBITDA margin improvement)

**Half-point scores (1.5, 2.5, 3.5, 4.5) are allowed** when a company falls between two levels.

### ⚠️ TIER 3 WEIGHTING: 3x MULTIPLIER

**Tier 3 categories (Customer Lifetime Value and New Products/Services) carry a 3x weight multiplier.** The raw score (1-5) is multiplied by 3 to produce the weighted contribution (3-15) to the total. This reflects the outsized strategic importance of revenue transformation in the AI investment thesis.

- Raw score 1 → weighted contribution = 3
- Raw score 2 → weighted contribution = 6
- Raw score 3 → weighted contribution = 9
- Raw score 4 → weighted contribution = 12
- Raw score 5 → weighted contribution = 15

**This multiplier applies to BOTH AI and Non-AI scores for Tier 3 categories.**

### ⚠️ TIER 3 SCORING RIGOR: CALIBRATED PE PARTNER STANDARD

**Because Tier 3 scores carry 3x weight, you MUST apply a rigorous, evidence-based standard when scoring Customer Lifetime Value and New Products/Services.** A score of 4 or 5 in these categories has an outsized impact on the total (12 or 15 weighted points), so high scores require strong supporting evidence.

**Calibration guidance for Tier 3 scoring:**
1. **Calibrate, don't default.** Score 1 (Minimal) should be reserved for companies with genuinely no AI-relevant customer data, no identifiable pricing variance, no cross-sell opportunity, and no adjacent market potential. For a typical mid-market PE company with a meaningful customer base, some operational data, and identifiable growth levers, the starting point for evaluation should be Score 2. Companies with demonstrable customer data assets, measurable churn, identifiable pricing variance, or concrete adjacent market opportunities should be evaluated for Score 3. The goal is accurate assessment, not systematic skepticism.
2. **Demand specific, concrete evidence for Score 4-5.** Vague statements like "AI could improve retention" or "data monetization is possible" are NOT sufficient for Score 4+. You need specific data points: customer base size, churn rates, pricing variance, proprietary data assets, adjacent market validation. Score 3 requires identifiable data assets and a plausible AI mechanism — not proven deployment or existing AI capabilities.
3. **Apply the "so what?" test.** For each piece of evidence, ask: "So what? Does this actually translate to measurable EBITDA impact through AI, or is it aspirational?" If you can't articulate the specific AI mechanism and quantify the margin impact, score lower.
4. **Challenge your own reasoning.** Before finalizing any Tier 3 score ≥4, write a 1-sentence counter-argument for why the score should be lower. If the counter-argument reveals a fundamental flaw in the evidence (e.g., the data doesn't actually exist, the market is structurally blocked, or regulatory barriers prevent execution), lower the score. General uncertainty, lack of existing AI deployment, or absence of a proof-of-concept is NOT sufficient reason to lower — this is a *potential* assessment, not a *proven results* assessment.
5. **Compare to calibration benchmarks.** A Score 5 in CLV means the company has a large, identifiable customer base (>50K), measurable churn (>10%), identifiable pricing variance (>15%), AND rich transaction/behavioral data that AI models can act on. This is achievable for non-tech companies in services, healthcare, consumer, and multi-location businesses — not only SaaS companies.
6. **Triple-check before submitting.** Re-read the decision rules for the score level you chose. Does the company genuinely meet ALL required criteria (for Score 4-5) or the minimum threshold (for Score 2-3)? Ensure scoring reflects the realistic potential a well-resourced PE owner could unlock within a 3-5 year hold period.

**Total AI Score** = Sum of 5 unweighted category AI scores + sum of 2 Tier 3 weighted AI scores. Range: 11-55.
**Total Non-AI Score** = Sum of 5 unweighted category Non-AI scores + sum of 2 Tier 3 weighted Non-AI scores. Range: 11-55.

**Formula:** vcTotalScore = CS + ISD + BO + SC + S&M + (CLV × 3) + (NP/S × 3)

**Decision Rule (AI Score — used for matrix classification):**
- **Low (11-25)**: Not applicable or no material improvement potential through AI interventions
- **Medium (26-40)**: Moderate economic improvement potential through AI interventions
- **High (41-55)**: Significant economic improvement potential through AI interventions

The Non-AI score provides context on whether value creation is primarily AI-driven or traditional-PE-driven. When Non-AI >> AI, the investment thesis rests on traditional levers with AI as supplementary. When AI >> Non-AI, AI is the defining thesis.

## Critical: AI Attribution Principle

**When scoring AI Value Creation Potential, assess only the INCREMENTAL value that AI specifically enables — not the total operational improvement opportunity.**

Many companies have significant value creation potential from traditional PE levers (operational excellence, consolidation, pricing discipline, procurement savings, management upgrades). The AI Score must isolate what AI uniquely adds beyond these traditional interventions.

**Ask for each category:** "If the company implemented best-practice operational improvements WITHOUT AI, how much additional EBITDA margin impact would AI unlock?"

- If AI is THE primary enabler (e.g., route optimization at scale requiring ML, predictive churn models using behavioral data), the AI-specific margin impact is large → score 5 (Transformative)
- If AI meaningfully enhances a traditional lever (e.g., AI-augmented pricing on top of basic pricing discipline), the margin impact is moderate → score 3 (Moderate)
- If the improvement is primarily achievable through traditional means and AI is marginal → score 1 (Minimal)
- If AI shows some promise but doesn't fully meet the Moderate threshold → score 2 (Low)
- If AI exceeds Moderate but doesn't fully meet Transformative threshold → score 4 (High)

**This distinction is what separates a "High" VC potential (AI IS the thesis) from "Medium" (AI is one of several value drivers).**

## Confidence Scoring Framework

Every category score must include a confidence level (High / Medium / Low). Confidence reflects the quality and quantity of evidence supporting the score.

**High Confidence** — Assign when AT LEAST TWO of the following are available:
- Direct management interview data or management presentation with operational detail
- Detailed financial breakdown by function (e.g., cost-center-level P&L, headcount by department)
- Comparable transaction data showing AI impact in similar businesses
- Proprietary operational data (call volumes, route data, service records, customer data)
- Third-party market study or diligence report with relevant analysis

**Medium Confidence** — Assign when exactly ONE of the above sources is available, supplemented by:
- Public financial filings with some segment detail
- Industry reports with relevant benchmarks
- Press coverage or analyst reports describing operations
- Management team backgrounds suggesting operational maturity

**Low Confidence** — Assign when scoring is based primarily on:
- Sector-level heuristics and general industry knowledge
- Public information only (website, press releases, high-level financials)
- Analogies to comparable companies without direct data
- Assumptions about operational structure without verification

**Rule:** If more than 3 of 7 VC categories are scored at Low confidence, flag the overall assessment as "Preliminary — requires additional diligence data" in the executive summary.

## Sector Emphasis Mechanism

All 7 categories carry equal weight (1/7) in the total score to maintain comparability across companies. However, certain categories are disproportionately important for specific business types.

**After scoring, flag 1-2 categories as "Sector Emphasis" if they meet this test:** The category represents >25% of the company's total cost base or >25% of the total value creation opportunity.

When a Sector Emphasis category scores 5 (AI) or 5 (Non-AI), note in the rationale: "This category carries outsized strategic significance for this business type — the score of 5 here has disproportionate impact on the investment thesis."

When a Sector Emphasis category scores 1, note: "Despite being a critical function for this business, AI offers limited incremental value here — this is an important finding."

**Common sector emphasis patterns:**
- Field services / logistics companies → Supply Chain
- Consumer subscription businesses → Customer Lifetime Value
- BPO / services businesses → Customer Service, Back Office
- Technology companies → Internal Software Development, New Products/Services
- B2B companies with large sales forces → Sales & Marketing
- Multi-location retail / hospitality → Back Office, Customer Lifetime Value
- Platform / marketplace businesses → New Products/Services, Customer Lifetime Value

## TIER 1: COST OPTIMIZATION

### Cost-Gate Flexibility Note

Each Tier 1 category uses cost-gates (% of opex) as scoring thresholds. **If a company's cost structure does not meet the cost-gate for a given score level, but the value capture criteria are clearly and strongly met, the scorer may award one level above Minimal (i.e., Score 2) based on value capture evidence alone.** This prevents companies with lean cost structures from being systematically penalized when genuine AI optimization opportunity exists. The cost-gate remains the primary guide — this exception applies only when value capture evidence is compelling and specific.

### Category 1: Customer Service

#### AI Score Decision Rules

Each score level requires BOTH a cost-gate (standardized across all Tier 1 categories) AND category-specific value capture criteria.

**Score 5 (Transformative) — Cost-gate AND ALL value capture criteria must be true:**
- **Cost-gate:** Customer service spend is ≥30% of total company opex
- **Value capture — ALL of the following:**
  - <30% of customer interactions currently handled through digital self-service
  - >50% of inbound interactions are routine/repetitive (scheduling, billing, status, FAQs)
  - Rich interaction data exists (call logs, chat transcripts, CRM records) for AI training
  - AI chatbots, virtual agents, and intelligent routing can plausibly handle 40-60% of interactions (>8pp EBITDA margin impact)

**Score 4 (High) — Cost-gate AND AT LEAST THREE value capture criteria must be true:**
- **Cost-gate:** Customer service spend is ≥24% of total company opex
- **Value capture — AT LEAST THREE of the following:**
  - 30-50% of interactions are routine and automatable
  - Rich interaction data available for AI training
  - AI can plausibly automate 25-40% of interactions
  - Current self-service rate is <50% with clear automation opportunity
  - 5-8pp EBITDA margin impact achievable

**Score 3 (Moderate) — Cost-gate AND AT LEAST TWO value capture criteria must be true:**
- **Cost-gate:** Customer service spend is ≥18% of total company opex
- **Value capture — AT LEAST TWO of the following:**
  - 30-60% of interactions are routine and automatable
  - Some interaction data available for AI training
  - AI can augment human agents (real-time guidance, sentiment analysis, next-best-action)
  - 3-5pp EBITDA margin impact achievable

**Score 2 (Low) — Cost-gate AND at least one value capture indicator:**
- **Cost-gate:** Customer service spend is ≥12% of total company opex
- **Value capture — at least one of the following:**
  - 20-30% of interactions are routine
  - Limited but some interaction data available
  - AI could provide modest improvements (1-3pp EBITDA margin impact)

**Score 1 (Minimal) — ANY of the following are true:**
- Customer service spend is <6% of total company opex
- Interactions require deep human judgment (complex technical, emotional, or regulatory)
- Customer service is already >60% digital self-service
- Customer service function is primarily outsourced

#### Non-AI Score Decision Rules

**Score 5 (Transformative) — AT LEAST TWO of the following are true:**
- No standardized scripts, processes, or quality assurance program in place
- High agent turnover (>40% annually) with no structured onboarding/training
- No modern CRM system or knowledge base for agents
- Customer satisfaction scores are significantly below industry benchmarks (>10pp gap)
- >8pp EBITDA margin impact achievable through traditional CS improvements

**Score 4 (High) — AT LEAST TWO of the following are true:**
- Inconsistent process standardization across locations/teams
- Agent turnover is elevated (30-40% annually) with basic training programs
- CRM exists but significantly underutilized or outdated
- Customer satisfaction is 5-10pp below industry benchmarks
- 5-8pp EBITDA margin impact achievable through traditional improvements

**Score 3 (Moderate) — AT LEAST ONE of the following is true:**
- Some process standardization exists but inconsistent across locations/teams
- Agent training programs exist but are basic
- CRM exists but underutilized or outdated
- 3-5pp EBITDA margin impact achievable through traditional improvements

**Score 2 (Low) — Company shows some opportunity but doesn't meet threshold for Moderate:**
- Basic process standardization in place with minor inconsistencies
- Basic training programs exist with room for enhancement
- CRM system exists and partially utilized
- 1-3pp EBITDA margin impact achievable through traditional improvements

**Score 1 (Minimal):**
- Customer service processes are well-standardized and quality-monitored
- Agent training and performance management are mature
- Modern CRM and knowledge management in place
- Customer satisfaction is at or above industry benchmarks

### Category 2: Internal Software Development

#### AI Score Decision Rules

Each score level requires BOTH a cost-gate (standardized across all Tier 1 categories) AND category-specific value capture criteria.

**Score 5 (Transformative) — Cost-gate AND ALL value capture criteria must be true:**
- **Cost-gate:** Internal software development spend (engineering salaries, contractors, tooling) is ≥30% of total company opex
- **Value capture — ALL of the following:**
  - Company has >100 software developers/engineers in-house
  - Software is a core product or critical competitive enabler (not just back-office IT)
  - Development velocity is a key constraint on business growth or product competitiveness
  - AI coding assistants and automated testing can plausibly deliver >30% developer productivity gain (>8pp EBITDA margin impact)

**Score 4 (High) — Cost-gate AND ALL value capture criteria must be true:**
- **Cost-gate:** Internal software development spend is ≥24% of total company opex
- **Value capture — ALL of the following:**
  - Company has 50-100+ developers/engineers
  - Software is core product or competitive enabler
  - AI tools can deliver 20-30% productivity gains (5-8pp EBITDA margin impact)

**Score 3 (Moderate) — Cost-gate AND AT LEAST TWO value capture criteria must be true:**
- **Cost-gate:** Internal software development spend is ≥18% of total company opex
- **Value capture — AT LEAST TWO of the following:**
  - Company has 25-100 developers/engineers
  - Some custom software, internal tools, or data pipelines that AI can help build/maintain faster
  - Moderate vendor spend on custom development that AI could reduce
  - 3-5pp EBITDA margin impact from developer productivity gains

**Score 2 (Low) — Cost-gate AND at least one value capture indicator:**
- **Cost-gate:** Internal software development spend is ≥12% of total company opex
- **Value capture — at least one of the following:**
  - Company has 15-25 developers/engineers
  - Limited custom software development with some AI optimization potential
  - 1-3pp EBITDA margin impact from AI productivity gains

**Score 1 (Minimal) — ANY of the following are true:**
- Internal software development spend is <6% of total company opex
- Technology needs are met primarily through off-the-shelf solutions and vendor platforms
- Software is not a core product or competitive differentiator
- IT team is operational (maintenance, support) not developmental

#### Non-AI Score Decision Rules

**Score 5 (Transformative) — AT LEAST TWO of the following are true:**
- Development practices are outdated (no CI/CD, poor testing, manual deployments)
- Significant technical debt constraining product velocity
- High developer turnover or difficulty hiring due to poor tooling/practices
- Outsourced development that could be brought in-house more efficiently
- >8pp margin impact from traditional engineering improvements

**Score 4 (High) — AT LEAST TWO of the following are true:**
- Some development practices outdated (partial CI/CD, inconsistent testing)
- Moderate to significant technical debt
- Elevated developer turnover or hiring challenges
- 5-8pp margin impact from traditional engineering improvements

**Score 3 (Moderate) — AT LEAST ONE of the following is true:**
- Some modernization needed in development practices
- Moderate technical debt or tooling improvements possible
- 3-5pp margin impact from traditional engineering improvements

**Score 2 (Low) — Company shows some opportunity but doesn't meet threshold for Moderate:**
- Minor improvements needed in development practices
- Technical debt is manageable but could be optimized
- 1-3pp margin impact from traditional engineering improvements

**Score 1 (Minimal):**
- Development practices are modern and well-organized
- Technical debt is minimal
- Team is productive with good tooling

### Category 4: Supply Chain

**Score 3 (Moderate) — Cost-gate AND AT LEAST TWO value capture criteria must be true:**
- **Cost-gate:** Supply chain spend is ≥18% of total company opex
- **Value capture — AT LEAST TWO of the following:**

  - Basic optimization tools in place but ML could improve on them

**Score 2 (Low) — Cost-gate AND at least one value capture indicator:**

**Score 4 (High) — ALL of the following must be true:**

- Some AI optimization potential but limited scope
- 1-3pp EBITDA margin impact from AI-driven improvements

## TIER 3: REVENUE TRANSFORMATION (3× WEIGHT MULTIPLIER)

⚠️ **ALL Tier 3 categories carry a 3× weight multiplier.** A raw score of 3 contributes 9 weighted points; a raw 5 contributes 15. Because of this outsized impact, you MUST apply the **Calibrated PE Partner Standard** (defined in the Scoring Scale section above) to every Tier 3 score. Evaluate starting from Score 2 for typical mid-market PE companies. Demand concrete evidence for Score 4+. Write a counter-argument before finalizing any score ≥4.

### Category 6: Customer Lifetime Value

Assesses whether AI can fundamentally improve customer lifetime value through better retention, deeper engagement, dynamic pricing, and cross-sell/upsell optimization. This replaces the v3 "Revenue Increase Potential" category with a sharper focus on customer-level economics.

#### AI Score Decision Rules

⚠️ **CALIBRATED PE PARTNER STANDARD APPLIES.** This score is weighted 3× in the total. Before assigning Score 4+, you must: (1) cite specific data — customer base size, churn rate, pricing variance, cross-sell penetration, (2) articulate the specific AI mechanism that drives EBITDA impact, and (3) write a counter-argument for a lower score. If the counter-argument reveals a fundamental evidence flaw, score lower. Score 3 requires identifiable data assets, a plausible AI mechanism, and a reasonable EBITDA impact path — it does NOT require proven deployment.

**Score 5 (Transformative) — ALL of the following must be true:**
- Customer churn rate is >10% annually AND customer base is >50K (enough data for predictive models)
- Pricing is currently static or regionally inconsistent with >15% price variance across comparable segments
- Product/service catalog has >3 offerings per customer with cross-sell penetration <40%
- Transaction or behavioral data is available at the customer level for model training
- AI-driven dynamic pricing, churn prediction, lifetime value optimization, or recommendation engines can plausibly deliver >3% revenue uplift (>8pp EBITDA margin impact)

**Score 4 (High) — ALL of the following must be true:**
- Customer churn rate is 8-10%+ annually with customer base >25K
- Pricing has 10-15% variance with some optimization opportunity
- 2-3+ offerings per customer with 40-60% cross-sell penetration
- Good customer-level data available
- AI can deliver 5-8pp EBITDA margin impact

**Score 3 (Moderate) — AT LEAST TWO of the following are true:**
- Customer churn rate is 5-10% annually with a customer base large enough for basic prediction models (>10K)
- Some pricing optimization opportunity exists (5-15% variance across regions or segments)
- Cross-sell/upsell opportunity exists but is moderate (2-3 products, 40-60% penetration)
- AI adds meaningful incremental lift beyond traditional retention/pricing programs (3-5pp EBITDA margin impact)
- Company has identifiable customer-level data (transaction history, service records, interaction logs) that a well-resourced PE owner could leverage for AI-driven retention or pricing within a 3-5 year hold period

> **B2B Data Ecosystem Note:** Companies that do not sell directly to end consumers but operate large installed bases, sensor networks, or B2B platforms generating behavioral/transactional data from downstream users (e.g., 10,000+ connected devices, 100+ venue/location partners) should be evaluated on the richness of their *indirect* data asset. If the company has identifiable downstream user data flowing through its infrastructure — even if it doesn't "own" the end-customer relationship — this qualifies as an AI-relevant data foundation for CLV scoring. Evaluate the data moat, not the billing relationship.

**Score 2 (Low) — Company shows some opportunity but doesn't meet threshold for Moderate:**
- Customer churn rate is 3-5% annually or smaller customer base (10K-50K)
- Minor pricing optimization opportunity (<10% variance)
- Limited cross-sell opportunity
- 1-3pp EBITDA margin impact from AI-driven improvements

**Score 1 (Minimal) — ANY of the following are true:**
- Customer churn is <3% annually (limited prediction value)
- Pricing is regulated, contractual, or already optimized
- Product portfolio is narrow with limited cross-sell opportunity (<2 products)
- Revenue growth is primarily driven by physical expansion or M&A, not analytics
- Customer base is <10K (insufficient data for meaningful AI models)

#### Non-AI Score Decision Rules

**Score 5 (Transformative) — AT LEAST TWO of the following are true:**
- Pricing is fragmented across regions/branches with >15% variance AND no structured pricing discipline program in place
- No formal retention program exists despite >10% annual churn
- Sales team lacks structured playbooks, training programs, or performance management
- Cross-sell/upsell has not been systematically pursued (no bundling strategy, no customer lifecycle management)
- >8pp EBITDA margin impact achievable through traditional revenue levers

**Score 4 (High) — AT LEAST TWO of the following are true:**
- Pricing has 10-15% variance with limited centralized discipline
- Basic retention programs exist but are reactive
- Sales team has some training but gaps in structure/playbooks
- Cross-sell opportunity is identified but not systematically executed
- 5-8pp EBITDA margin impact achievable through traditional improvements

**Score 3 (Moderate) — AT LEAST ONE of the following is true:**
- Some pricing discipline exists but room for improvement (5-15% variance)
- Basic retention programs exist but are reactive, not proactive
- Sales effectiveness can be improved through traditional training and process
- 3-5pp EBITDA margin impact achievable through traditional means

**Score 2 (Low) — Company shows some opportunity but doesn't meet threshold for Moderate:**
- Pricing is mostly optimized with minor variance (< 5%)
- Retention programs exist with some proactive elements
- Sales team has basic training and processes
- 1-3pp EBITDA margin impact achievable through traditional improvements

**Score 1 (Minimal):**
- Pricing is already optimized and centrally managed
- Retention programs are mature with proactive outreach
- Sales team is well-trained with structured processes
- Revenue growth is primarily market-driven, not execution-driven

### Category 7: New Products/Services

Assesses whether AI can enable entirely new products, services, or revenue streams that do not currently exist in the company's portfolio. This is a new category in v4, capturing the transformative upside of AI beyond optimization of existing operations.

#### AI Score Decision Rules

⚠️ **CALIBRATED PE PARTNER STANDARD APPLIES.** This score is weighted 3× in the total. Before assigning Score 4+, you must: (1) identify specific proprietary data assets or domain knowledge, (2) define a concrete adjacent market or product opportunity with evidence of customer demand, and (3) write a counter-argument for a lower score. If the counter-argument reveals a fundamental evidence flaw, score lower. Score 3 requires identifiable data/domain assets and a plausible adjacent opportunity — it does NOT require proven deployment or market validation. "Data monetization potential" or "could build AI features" alone is NOT sufficient for Score 4+, but IS sufficient for Score 3 when paired with identifiable assets.

**Score 5 (Transformative) — ALL of the following must be true:**
- Company has proprietary data assets (customer data, operational data, domain-specific datasets) that can be monetized or used to build AI-powered products
- Adjacent market opportunities exist where AI-enabled offerings would be valued by existing customers or new segments
- The company's brand, distribution, and customer relationships provide a defensible go-to-market for new AI-powered offerings
- AI-enabled new products/services can plausibly generate >5% incremental revenue within 3-5 years (>8pp EBITDA margin impact when combined with high-margin new revenue)

**Score 4 (High) — ALL of the following must be true:**
- Company has substantial proprietary data assets with clear monetization potential
- Adjacent market opportunities are well-defined with customer validation
- Brand/distribution provides meaningful go-to-market advantage
- AI offerings could generate 3-5% incremental revenue (5-8pp EBITDA margin impact)

**Score 3 (Moderate) — AT LEAST TWO of the following are true:**
- Company has some proprietary data or domain-specific knowledge that could be leveraged for AI-enhanced or new offerings
- AI could enhance existing products/services in ways that justify premium pricing or expand TAM
- Some adjacent opportunities exist (even if market validation is early-stage)
- 3-5pp EBITDA margin impact from AI-augmented or new offerings
- Company has brand, distribution, or customer relationships that could support AI-enabled product extensions within a 3-5 year PE hold period

> **B2B Data Monetization Note:** For B2B companies, "New Products/Services" includes monetizing operational data as analytics, insights, or decision-support tools sold back to the company's direct customers (e.g., operator dashboards, predictive models, benchmarking services). A visible high-growth digital/interactive segment or demonstrable analytics platform counts as concrete evidence of new product traction, even if the company's core business is physical/hardware.

**Score 2 (Low) — Company shows some opportunity but doesn't meet threshold for Moderate:**
- Company has limited proprietary data or unclear data monetization path
- Minor adjacent market opportunities exist
- Some brand/distribution advantage but limited
- 1-3pp EBITDA margin impact from AI-augmented offerings

**Score 1 (Minimal) — ANY of the following are true:**
- Company has no meaningful proprietary data assets or domain-specific knowledge that AI could leverage
- Core business is purely operational with no product/service innovation history
- Existing customer base would not value AI-augmented offerings
- Regulatory constraints prevent new product development or data utilization
- Business model is commodity-driven with limited differentiation opportunity

#### Non-AI Score Decision Rules

**Score 5 (Transformative) — AT LEAST TWO of the following are true:**
- Clear whitespace opportunities exist in adjacent markets that don't require AI
- Company has under-leveraged brand or distribution that could support new offerings
- M&A or partnership opportunities could bring new products/services to market
- >8pp margin impact achievable through new revenue streams

**Score 4 (High) — AT LEAST TWO of the following are true:**
- Multiple adjacent market opportunities identified with some customer interest
- Brand/distribution could effectively support new offerings with investment
- M&A/partnership pipeline exists with potential targets identified
- 5-8pp margin impact achievable through new revenue streams

**Score 3 (Moderate) — AT LEAST ONE of the following is true:**
- Some adjacent opportunities exist but require investment and market development
- Product/service line extensions are possible through traditional means
- 3-5pp margin impact achievable through traditional new business development

**Score 2 (Low) — Company shows some opportunity but doesn't meet threshold for Moderate:**
- Limited adjacent opportunities with significant investment required
- Minor brand/distribution advantage for new offerings
- 1-3pp margin impact achievable through new business development

**Score 1 (Minimal):**
- Business is mature with limited new product/service opportunity
- Market is saturated with limited whitespace
- Company lacks the brand, distribution, or capabilities for new offerings

## Aggregate Scoring Methodology

**Total AI Score** = Sum of 5 unweighted AI scores + (CLV AI × 3) + (NP/S AI × 3). Range: 11-55.
**Total Non-AI Score** = Sum of 5 unweighted Non-AI scores + (CLV Non-AI × 3) + (NP/S Non-AI × 3). Range: 11-55.

**Formula:** vcTotalScore = CS + ISD + BO + SC + S&M + (CLV × 3) + (NP/S × 3)

**Decision Rule (Value Creation Potential):**
- **Low (11-25)**: AI is not a material value driver. Traditional PE levers dominate.
- **Medium (26-40)**: AI is one of several meaningful value creation levers. Worth pursuing but not the defining thesis.
- **High (41-55)**: AI transformation could be the defining value creation thesis. Significant EBITDA margin improvement achievable.

**AI vs. Non-AI Comparison:**
- **Non-AI >> AI (difference ≥8)**: Traditional PE thesis. AI is supplementary upside.
- **Non-AI ≈ AI (difference ≤7)**: Balanced thesis. AI is meaningful alongside traditional levers.
- **AI >> Non-AI (difference ≥8)**: AI-led thesis. AI is the defining value driver.

## Scoring Process Checklist

For each category, the scorer must:
1. Review the quantitative decision rules and check which threshold is met
2. Assign the AI score (1-5, with half-points allowed) with specific evidence cited
3. Assign the Non-AI score (1-5, with half-points allowed) with specific evidence cited
4. Assign a confidence level (High/Medium/Low) using the Confidence Framework above
5. Write a 2-3 sentence rationale citing specific data points
6. Flag if the category qualifies as Sector Emphasis
7. Note any key assumptions that could change the score
8. **FOR TIER 3 CATEGORIES ONLY:** Apply the Calibrated PE Partner Standard (see above). Before finalizing any score ≥4, write a counter-argument for why the score should be lower. If the counter-argument reveals a fundamental evidence flaw, lower the score.

**Note on Half-Point Scoring:** Half-point scores (1.5, 2.5, 3.5, 4.5) are allowed and encouraged when a company falls between two levels. For example, score 2.5 when the company shows characteristics of both Score 2 (Low) and Score 3 (Moderate) but the evidence doesn't firmly support either level alone.

**Note on Tier 3 Weighted Totals:** When computing vcTotalScore and vcNonAiTotalScore, remember to multiply CLV and NP/S raw scores by 3. The JSON `scoring_data.json` stores the RAW scores (1-5) in the category objects, but vcTotalScore and vcNonAiTotalScore must reflect the WEIGHTED totals (range 11-55).
